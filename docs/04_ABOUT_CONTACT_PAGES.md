### Section 1: Who We Are (300 words)

**Headline:** "We Are BaliGO – Your Local Connection to Bali"

**Content:**
BaliGO was born from a simple belief: tourism should benefit locals, not replace them.

In 2019, our founder Ketut Dharma—a third-generation Balinese guide—recognized a problem. International tourism platforms were flooding Bali with tour operators who prioritized volume over experience, extracting profits from the island without giving back. Tourists wanted authenticity; locals needed fair compensation and respect. Neither was happening.

So Ketut created BaliGO—not a corporate tour company, but a community platform connecting passionate travelers with genuine local guides, artisans, and small businesses.

**What Makes Us Different:**

- **100% Local Ownership:** BaliGO is operated by Balinese people who live here, who know every corner, every story, every cultural nuance.
- **Fair Compensation:** Unlike mainstream tour operators, 85% of revenue goes directly to guides, artisans, and local communities. We believe in fair wages and direct relationships.
- **Authentic Experiences:** We don't offer manufactured tourism. Our guides share real Bali—the spirituality, the humor, the complexity, the beauty—because that's where they live.
- **Small Groups:** You'll never be herded into a 40-person tour bus. Our maximum group size is 12, usually smaller. This means genuine connections, personalized attention, and less environmental impact.
- **Quality Over Quantity:** We carefully vet every experience and guide. Our 4.9★ rating from 2,000+ travelers isn't luck—it's commitment.

Today, BaliGO has grown to 50+ full-time and part-time guides, artisans, and community partners. But our core remains unchanged: connecting people authentically, supporting Bali's communities, and proving that tourism can be ethical and extraordinary simultaneously.

### Section 2: Our Mission & Vision (250 words)

**Headline:** "Why We Do What We Do"

**Mission Statement:**
To create transformative travel experiences that authentically connect visitors with Balinese culture, communities, and nature—while ensuring economic benefits flow directly to local guides, artisans, and families.

**Vision Statement:**
A Bali where tourism strengthens—rather than displaces—local communities; where travelers leave as educated advocates for cultural preservation and sustainable practices; where guides and artisans are respected professionals, not interchangeable service providers.

**Our Core Values:**

1. **Authenticity** – We celebrate real Bali, not theme park versions. Your guide will share genuine aspects of their life, culture, and community. You'll see the Bali that locals know and love.

2. **Community** – Every booking supports local livelihoods. Our guides are teachers, artists, farmers, spiritual practitioners. They're using tourism income to support families, educate children, preserve traditions.

3. **Sustainability** – We limit group sizes, choose eco-friendly practices, educate travelers about environmental impact, and support marine/forest conservation efforts. Tourism should heal the island, not harm it.

4. **Respect** – We respect Balinese spiritual practices, cultural values, and boundaries. Sacred sites are treated as sacred. Local customs aren't photo opportunities; they're lived reality.

5. **Excellence** – Your experience matters. We invest in guide training, carefully curate offerings, and listen to feedback. A 4.9★ rating reflects our commitment to doing it right.
